SPOTLIGHT Shanghai, China

The Bund

Features

A striking waterfront promenade stretching along the western bank of the Huangpu River. It offers an unparalleled visual contrast between the historic architectural skyline on one side and the futuristic skyscrapers of Lujiazui on the other.

Historical Origins

Often referred to as the "Museum of International Architecture," The Bund houses dozens of historical buildings showcasing Romanesque, Gothic, Renaissance, and Art Deco styles, symbolizing Shanghai's golden era in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Recommended To Do

Take an evening stroll to witness the spectacular neon light shows reflecting across the river, or book a twilight river cruise for a panoramic, unobstructed view of both sides of the city.
